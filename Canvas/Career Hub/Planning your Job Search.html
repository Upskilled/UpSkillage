Job Readiness / Planning your job search

Planning your job search

Once you are ready to get a job, it is time to come up with a plan for your job search. The first step is to organise your documents. We recommend creating a folder on your computer with a subfolder for resumes and for cover letters and a spreadsheet to track your job search. Once you have these in order you will be ready to begin applying for jobs.

Master resume template

Using the resume section of the career hub, we recommend beginning with a master resume template that will make your life a whole lot easier. Before you have a specific job in mind, use the template we've attached to build out a resume for yourself that highlights your core skills. You may not ever send this exact resume to potential employers, but it's much easier to change a few bullets each time you find a new job than to start your resume from scratch.

Master cover letter template

Next we recommend doing the same for your cover letter. It may be a very generic letter but again it will make your life a whole lot easier if you can just update the basic cover letter with specifics for that position rather than starting from scratch each time.

Application organisation

Your job search can get overwhelming especially when you begin applying for 10, 20 or even 30 jobs at once. To help keep you organised we recommend keeping a spreadsheet of all the jobs you have applied for and what stage each one is at. Below is a template for you to make a copy of to help organise your job search.

Steps

  1. When you apply for a new job add them to the sheet and list out the information.
  2. Update the sheet when you hear back from employers or after a prolonged period without a response (say 1 month).
  3. Set a weekly time to look through the spreadsheet and evaluate your job search strategy.
