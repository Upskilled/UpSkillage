Utilising LinkedIn

In the modern job search you can pretty much guarantee that a recruiter will search for your LinkedIn profile before they invite you to an interview. This means its critical to:

  1. Have a LinkedIn
  2. Have a LinkedIn that shows off your abilities
  3. Use LinkedIn to substantiate and supplement your resume

When starting off on LinkedIn, you want to make sure you cover three basic concepts. You want your LinkedIn to be professional, complete and highlight your best achievements. Unlike your resume, you will only have one, so it's ok to be a bit more broad and highlight your past achievements. That being said, if you know what industry you want to go into, you can optimise your profile in a very similar way by including keywords and relevant skills.

Professionalism

Before diving into how to make a LinkedIn, it's important to understand what LinkedIn is. LinkedIn is a social media platform with the key distinction that it is for professional purposes. This doesn't mean hide your personality, it just means avoid using it as a regular social media platform. Avoid using slang words, goofy pictures or making political posts. After all, your future employers are going to be the ones viewing your profile. You want to showcase that you are presentable, professional and most importantly that you aren't going to be a liability if they hire you.
