JOB READINESS / INTERVIEW PREP

Personal elevator pitch

Most interviews, no matter the job or company, will begin with the interviewer saying - Tell me about yourself.

When you hear this, you should be prompted to give them your personal elevator pitch. The pitch should be no more than 1 minute long and is your opportunity to explain your career and highlight what makes you an exceptional candidate. It is critical that you practice and know what you are going to say so that you can make the best impression possible.

 

Pitch notes

  • Keep the pitch to 1 minute maximum.
  • Start the pitch from when you began your career and go through your career.
  • Explain why you made the changes you did and the things you liked about each part of your career. (feel free to leave out unimportant bits)
  • Keep the pitch positive and be sure to highlight awards and achievements that will stand out to the interviewer.
  • Show your personality and that you are a normal person with comments like “then I decided I wanted to start a family".
  • Articulate how your career has led to you wanting the job you are applying for.
  • Add in personal interests that show who you really are.

STARL

Throughout your job search you will be asked questions in a number of different settings. Oftentimes employers will ask you to answer specific questions in a cover letter or resume. Additionally, government jobs and a growing number of corporations will ask you to fill out Key Selection Criteria (KSC) which are questions to gage your fitness for the role.

When answering these questions you want to make sure that your answers are well structured and easy for the audience to follow. As such we recommend using the STARL model.

The STARL model is used around the world as an excellent way to answer any question. The model allows you to explain your answer clearly and give the audience exactly what it is they are looking for. If you are ever given a question that starts with “Tell me about a time when” or “Can you give me an example of'', use the STARL model to answer that question.

Answer interview questions using the STARL model:

  • Situation - Begin with describing the facts of the situation and what happened.
  • Task - Next describe the problem and the task you had to complete to solve it.
  • Action - From there explain exactly what you did and how you did it.
  • Result - Explain the outcome your action had on the team or the organisation.
  • Learning - Talk about how you took what happened in this instance and used it to make better decisions and take better action in the future.
