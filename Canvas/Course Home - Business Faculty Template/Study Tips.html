Study tips

You have joined the Upskilled Learning Community and in order to be a successful online learner, it is important that you become an active participant in your learning community.

To achieve this, we encourage you to:

  • Share information about yourself.
  • Post a photo of yourself in your profile.
  • Post replies in discussion forums.
  • Share your ePortfolio page with your trainer.
  • Discussion Forums - participation is vital, post 1 or 2 times per forum - use clear and concise language.
  • Respond to other student questions/forum posts.
  • Post questions yourself.
  • Be willing to work with others online.
  • Share your work/life experiences related to the topics online.
  • Reach out for help and study buddies for role plays.

It’s a good idea to set weekly study goals, this may mean committing to a minimum number of hours, dedicating specific time each week where you focus on your course, reading content, watching videos, taking notes, reviewing assessments, attending Ask Me Anything sessions and posting in discussion forums.

Use all of the tools Upskilled is providing you such as Trainer Contact via Ask Me Anything sessions, Discussion Forums where you can seek help from other students, use your ePortfolios as a digital notepad to self reflect and draft responses, watch the videos to explore the content material, conduct additional research activities and complete the various inbuilt learning activities.

By using all the tools at your disposal during your weekly study time, you are preparing yourself to complete assessments. If you have a question, reach out to your learning community, no need to feel isolated or alone, help is available via an email, a forum post or attendance at one of our many Ask Me Anything sessions.

Look out for Announcements from your trainer notifying you of when these are held or view the Course Calendar to register your attendance at one of these AMA sessions.
