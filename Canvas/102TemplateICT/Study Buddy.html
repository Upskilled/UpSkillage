Study buddy

We invite you to connect with your fellow students in your course to discuss course topics, practice and participate in role-plays, and or support one another in your course. Please reach out in the discussion forums or contact your Trainer for help to find a Study Buddy.

 

Study Buddy code of conduct

Important: Please note that Trainers and Assessors and other members of Upskilled such as the Head of Faculty have access and will review all communication. In this respect, it is important that students communicate appropriately. For example, discussions are not a forum where you would discuss the 'answers' to assessment questions, though you can discuss the general requirements of assessments together. Forums present an opportunity for students to discuss and support one another in their course, for instance, you might want to practice and facilitate role-plays, or work together on how to approach the different areas of assessments.

Privacy and Confidentiality: The discussions are not forums where you should be discussing in-depth personal information about yourself or other people. For example, you should not be discussing your stories or other people's stories from the past.

There is an Upskilled Student Code of Conduct that all students must adhere to, this includes all communications with all members of Upskilled. It is important to treat one another with courtesy and respect. You will find the Upskilled Student Code of Conduct here: Code of Conduct

Generally, you should follow these guidelines.

Students will:

  1. Not share personal information (their own or others, consider anything that is said in the online forums as strictly confidential).
  2. Respect each other’s ideas.
  3. Critique ideas, not people.
  4. Use appropriate language.
  5. Respect the diversity of the group.
  6. Keep on task (limit talk about non-related events).
  7. Take responsibility for the quality of the discussion.
  8. Contact Trainer for any course issues or concerns (refer to communication channels for support)

Guidelines for Study Buddies:

Have respect for each other.

  • Respect each other’s ideas
  • Don’t interrupt each other
  • Everyone’s opinion should count
  • Be honest with each other
  • Don’t take over and don’t let others take over
  • Help each other to understand all concepts
  • Be willing to cooperate with others on their ideas
  • Keep an open mind

Communication:

  • Listen effectively
  • Don’t be critical

Time management.

  • Attend and arrive on time to meetings
  • Be flexible about meeting times
  • Keep on task (limit talk about non-related events)
