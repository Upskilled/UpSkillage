Student group rules and code of conduct

Important: Please note that Trainers and Assessors and other members of Upskilled such as the Head of Faculty have access and will review all communication within the Student Groups. In this respect, it is important that students use the Student Groups tool appropriately. For example, Student Groups are not a forum where you would discuss the 'answers' to assessment questions, though you can discuss the general requirements of assessments together. The Student Study Groups present an opportunity for students to discuss and support one another in their course, for instance, you might want to practice and facilitate the practical areas of your course such as role-plays, including working together on how to approach the different areas of these assessments.

Privacy and Confidentiality: The student study groups are not forums where you should be discussing in-depth personal information about yourself or other people. For example, you should not be discussing your stories or clients or other peoples stories from the past. If you would like to discuss these things with one another, it would be best to create a discussion outside of the Upskilled education environment.

There is an Upskilled Student Code of Conduct that all students must adhere to, this includes all communications with all members of Upskilled. It is important to treat one another with courtesy and respect. You will find the Upskilled Student Code of Conduct here: Code of Conduct

Generally, you should follow these guidelines.

Students will:

  1. Not share personal information (their own or others, consider anything that is said in the online forums as strictly confidential to your group).
  2. Respect each other’s ideas.
  3. Critique ideas, not people.
  4. Use appropriate language.
  5. Respect the diversity of the group.
  6. Keep on task (limit talk about non-related events).
  7. Take responsibility for the quality of the discussion.
  8. Contact Trainer for any course issues or concerns (refer to communication channels for support)

 

Guidelines for groups:

Have respect for each other.

  • Respect each other’s ideas
  • Respect the other group members
  • Don’t interrupt each other
  • Everyone’s opinion should count
  • Be honest with each other

All group members should do an equal amount of work.

  • Everyone should share the responsibility of the tasks
  • Don’t take over and don’t let others take over

Your group should have a common understanding of goals that need to be achieved.

  • Help each other to understand all concepts

Be open to compromise.

  • Be willing to cooperate with others on their ideas
  • Keep an open mind
  • Vote on disagreements

Communicate effectively.

  • Make sure everyone is able to be vocal about their ideas and problems
  • Give ideas no matter how “off” you may think they are
  • Listen effectively
  • Don’t be critical

Time management.

  • Attend and arrive on time to all group meetings
  • Be flexible about meeting times
  • Keep on task (limit talk about non-related events)

Be happy in the group you are in.

 

Creating or joining a group

If you click on the Available Groups button above you will find the groups available that you can join. If the Group has an option to 'join' you can click on this to join that particular Group. If there is no option to 'join', then it may be that the particular group is full and the Group owner has turned off this option. In this case, you might want to create your own group if there are no other groups available for the activities you want to achieve.

You can create a group in the Student Groups section by clicking on the +Group button at the top left hand of the Student Group Page.

You should create a name for your Group that is relevant to the activities that will take place within that Group i.e. 'Jenny's Develop workplace communication strategies Role Play practice group'.

 

Inviting other students to your group

If you have created a student study group, you might want to advertise that group to other students. You can do that by adding the name of your group with a friendly invitation to the discussion here or in the Student Lobby section and asking for people to join. Contact your Trainer if you are having trouble with engaging with other students in Student Groups.

 

Volunteer yourself for groups

We encourage students to volunteer themselves in joining any Student Groups, particularly for practising role-plays and other practical applications. You may, for instance, volunteer for role-plays in sections of your course that you have not yet reached. This will give you valuable experience in being involved in different types of role-plays as well as understanding the requirements of that area when it is your turn to facilitate the assessment yourself. The Student Group area is a forum where students are able to support one another to discuss different areas of their course, practice skills, and prepare for practical assessment tasks.

 

Record your practical assessments for submission: Zoom video sessions including recording for assessments

Record your Practical Assessments for submission: Zoom Video sessions including recording for assessments

There is a Zoom section on the Home Page of your course where you can download your own private Zoom account, that is if you don't already have an account. You can invite other students to your Zoom meeting if you wish to record it by presenting the Zoom invite within your Student Group section as an announcement or discussion within the Student Group. Zoom sessions can be recorded and as such Zoom is a great tool to capture the recorded role-plays required as assessment submissions in your course. You should use your own private Zoom account if you wish to 'record' a meeting with other students. This can be used when you are recording a practical assessment such as a role-play, for that purpose the recording needs to be saved to your computer so that it can be uploaded as an assessment submission when required.
