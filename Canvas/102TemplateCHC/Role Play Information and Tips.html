Information for facilitating role plays in Upskilled assessments

Why role plays?

Role playing is a tool used by trainers and assessors to assess students and their capacity to demonstrate knowledge and skills by playing a particular role that relates to the area they are studying. i.e. a Case Manager in the Community Services industry. In this respect it is important that students have given themselves enough time to be well prepared for each role play and what is expected of them in this role to be facilitated in an assessment environment.

Preparation

It is recommended that students will have completed all necessary preparation including research of the required areas they are facilitating before conducting their role play assessment/s. Students need to ensure that they fully understand what is required of them to address all areas as described within the role play assessment/s.

Preparation could include:

  • studying the assessment requirements and related documents in detail,
  • practicing the role play in the student’s course with other students, with work colleagues, friends or family members,
  • consulting with their trainer and assessor so that they are familiar with how they will go about facilitating that role and its requirements competently.

Preparing role play participants

It is necessary for students to be clear about who will be playing the other part/s within the role play/s (clients etc.) and how they will prepare them for this. This is extremely important as the role play participants will need to be well informed and play their part competently for the student to facilitate their role and address the required areas of the assessment.

Ensuring role play participants are well prepared could include:

  • providing them with all information necessary on the role play part and discussing this with them (such as a Case Scenario) so that they are aware of the specifics of their role within the role play.
  • The student could also practice the role play with the role play participants before the role play takes place and
  • be available for any questions the role play participant may have.

Role play facilitation

Students should be confident in their ability if they are well prepared and have prepared the role play participants as required. Assessors are looking to see that students can competently cover the areas in the assessment instructions within the allotted time frame. At the time of facilitating the actual role play/ students need to ensure their role play is competent, focused and clear, addressing all required areas within that time frame.

Role playing and interactive learning activities

This course comes with Interactive Learning Activities. Upskilled understands the importance of practical experience, so to complement the theory components of your course, our specialist trainers may run ad-hoc Interactive Learning Activities.

You'll have the opportunity to collaborate with other students - sharing questions, thoughts and ideas, as well as participating together in interactive learning activities.

While the Interactive Learning Activities are not mandatory, your participation is encouraged.

You can receive some additional benefits and support, which may include:

  • Having a study buddy where you can bounce off ideas and help each other with assessments.
  • Online Classrooms where our specialist trainers can go over learning content that's part of your course.
  • Activities that relate to your assessments, which can help boost your knowledge and skills.

Additionally, role-playing is included in some of the learning areas of this course. It will also make up some of the practical elements of your assessments, including the assessments in your work placement.

The benefits of role playing

Upskilled believes that you can benefit from undertaking role-playing activities throughout your studies. Not only are they fun and interactive, it gives you the opportunity to collaborate with other participants such as other students, your family, friends and or colleagues.

Below are some benefits you can experience when role-playing:

Improved decision making and communication skills

Working in health and community services may involve you working in a variety of work settings. Professionals in this field are expected to make decisions and communicate effectively with clients, colleagues and stakeholders. Our role-playing assessments can help further improve your decision-making and communication skills in a community service workplace.

Preparation for work placement

Role-playing is effective in helping you prepare for work placement. Before you go on work placement, you'll need to complete most of the theory components of your course. The skills and knowledge developed from your role-playing assessments are based on scenarios you may face when working in the community services industry.

Develop knowledge and skills to be "work-ready"

Due to the interactive nature of role-playing, it can be an opportunity to practice community service industry roles with volunteers and other students to enhance your knowledge and skills. Job roles within this field are "hands-on", so role-playing can prepare you for the physical nature of the work - particularly in the disability and aged care sectors.
